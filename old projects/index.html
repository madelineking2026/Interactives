Kansas City pummeled with worst snow in decades

By Madeline King

The Kansas City metropolitan area was hit with over 11 inches of snow this week, which — according to meteorologist Matt Miller — marks the most snowfall the region has seen in over three decades.

Residents in affected areas saw road closures for extended periods of time. Some citizens did not leave their house for days. Others did decide to drive in the weather, risking weather-related car crashes that remained frequent throughout the week.

In addition to road closures, residents of the area were also burdened by restrictions to air travel. According to regional officials, local airports experienced:

  • 370 flight cancelations
  • 60 flight delays

Many homes within the broader metropolitan area experienced power outages, hitting civilians particularly hard in a week plagued by road closures.
